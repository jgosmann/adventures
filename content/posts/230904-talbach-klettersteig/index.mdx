I am spending a couple of days in the Zillertal with my parents. After arriving
yesterday afternoon, it is our first actual day of activities today. We start
with the Talbach via ferrata, graded C/D with an optional short D/E spot.

<Loc latlong={[47.22681, 11.86037]} zoom={12} />

The via ferrata starts with a quite loose and shaky rope bridge.

After about 70 minutes and an ascent of 175m, we complete the via ferrata and
return to our holiday flat in Mayrhofen for lunch. It is just a short trip with
the narrow-gauge railway running through the valley, the Zillertalbahn.

<Gallery>
  <Rimg src="z.jpeg" caption="The Zillertalbahn." />
  <Rimg src="26.jpeg" caption="The building with our holiday flat." />
</Gallery>

In the afternoon, we decide to do the Huterlaner via ferrata (C/D) near
Mayrhofen and continue from there with the Astegg via ferrata (C, variant with
D/E). The Huterlaner has some nice corners to climb. The Astegg via ferrata is
a lot of walking and mostly very easy with some a few hard(er) stretches.

<Loc latlong={[47.173, 11.8597]} zoom={12} />

I like the D/E variant of the Astegg. It requires to work with the rock
structures as there are no artificial foot holds except for the anchors of the
steel cable.
