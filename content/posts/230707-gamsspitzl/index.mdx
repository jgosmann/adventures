start to descend a few hundred meters to the Obersulzbach lake. It is quite
steep and in parts we climb down an easy via ferrata.

After a while, we get to the Krimmlertörlkees glaciar, where we rope up. At
first it is flat, but then gets increasingly steeper until we leave the glacier
at the ridge line near the Gamsspitzl (2888m).

<Rimg
  src="38.jpeg"
  caption="View back to the Kürsingerhütte and the Großvenediger to the right. You get a good view of the North ridge."

On top of the Gamsspitzl, we have a long break
and enjoy the sun and view for a few hours while our mountain guides Franz and
Martin already descend to do some scouting for the next day. Some hikers, coming
up after us, joke that the summit is inhabited.

Eventually, we also hike down to the Warnsdorfer Hütte. In total we descended
875m today, and ascended only 660m. It took us 4:30h of walking.

<Rimg src="65.jpeg" caption="Hike down to the Warnsdorfer Hütte." />

The hut turns out to be quite
luxurious. We get our own room with proper beds, sockets next to the beds, and
all seemingly refurbished not long ago. There is still a strong, but pleasant
smell of stone pine in the air.

<Rimg src="74.jpeg" caption="Warnsdorfer Hütte." />

We spend the remainder of the afternoon on the terrace, watching marmots (they
had some young ones too!), and eating
[Kaiserschmarrn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaiserschmarrn). The portion
turned out quite huge, so we ended up packing part of it for the next day.
