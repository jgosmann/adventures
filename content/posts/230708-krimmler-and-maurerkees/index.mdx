Another day of waking up early at 4am. Breakfast is served in the hut's seminar
room for us. It turns out to be quite modern, has a climbing wall and facilities
to practice crevasse rescue.

<Rimg src="1.jpeg" caption="Breakfast in the hut's seminar room." />

At 5 am, we head towards the Krimmler Kees glacier and start to ascend it.
Apparently, we are not following a common route, but the mountain guide is
making up his own path. We expected the steep ascent to be quite strenuous, but
on the third day of carrying heavy backpacks at altitude our bodies have finally
adjusted and acclimatized. The ascent turns out to be much more comfortable than
we expected. We also get some great views.

After a final steep bit, we make it onto the ridge line that separates the
Krimmlerkees from the Maurerkees glacier. This also brings us out of the shade
into the sun, where it is comfortably warm. We take break here. We could ascend
the Mittlerer Maurerkeeskopf (3283m) from here, but decide against it to save
some time and hopefully have better snow on the way down. The sun will soon turn
it into slush, that won't be nice to walk on (though going down on it is still
better than going up).

We arrive at the hut before noon. After some drinks, it is time to say goodbye
to Franz and Martin. With the warm temperatures and even warmer temperatures
tomorrow, conditions are not suitable anymore for another glacier tour. Margret,
Fabian, and I spent a relaxed afternoon at the hut. For dinner they do
a barbecue buffet with live traditional music. Unfortunately, it rains in the
evening so they have to shift everything inside.
